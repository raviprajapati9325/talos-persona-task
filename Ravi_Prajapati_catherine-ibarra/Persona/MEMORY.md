# Memory: Catherine Ibarra

## Personal Profile

- Born in Split to a Spanish father; raised in a household where Spanish was the language of the kitchen and Croatian the language of the neighborhood. Croatian citizen (EU).
- Education: Ph.D. Marine Ecology, University of the Adriatic Coast (2010); M.Sc. Marine Biology, Littoral University of Sciences, Rijeka (2005); B.Sc. Biology, University of the Adriatic Coast (2002).
- Languages: Spanish (native), Croatian (native), English (fluent), Portuguese (conversational), basic French (reading). Spanish is her private register, Croatian her public one, English her professional one; each accesses a slightly different version of herself.
- Her father Alejandro came to Split from Spain as a young man and died in 2021. Maintaining the bridge between Spanish heritage and Croatian life now falls to her, through food, the gajeta, and Spanish-language calls with her mother. Grief sits quietly alongside everything; she approaches it through practical details rather than the emotional landscape underneath.
- Temperament: economical with words, deeply observant (fieldwork trained her to notice what untrained eyes miss, in water and in people), and self-sufficient by design rather than guarded. She files observations and waits to see if they matter. Once she forms an assessment, she requires substantial evidence to revise it, which makes her rigorous professionally and occasionally unfair to first impressions.
- An introvert who recharges alone and does not experience the absence of a romantic partner as a deficit needing correction. Her life is a house furnished to her own specifications.
- Philosophy: the natural world has intrinsic value independent of human utility, and documenting and protecting it is not optional. Competence is a form of integrity: do the work well because the work matters, not because someone is watching. Solitude is a skill worth cultivating, not a condition to remedy. She respects institutions as both enablers and constrainers of the work, and treats their bureaucracy as the overhead cost of doing science. Family loyalty runs on love, not duty.

## Work & Projects

- Employer: University of the Adriatic Coast, Department of Marine Sciences, a public research university in Split. Title: Senior Research Scientist (Viši znanstveni suradnik), a permanent post.
- **Posidonia Oceanica Monitoring Network (POMN)**: Long-term monitoring of seagrass meadow health across 14 Adriatic sites, run on seasonal diving surveys and water quality sampling. Croatian Science Foundation funding of €120,000 over three years; currently Year 2 of 3. Catherine is PI.
- **MedSeagrass Horizon Europe Project**: EU-funded multi-country research on Mediterranean seagrass ecosystem services; 8 partners across 4 countries, kickoff held in Naples in February 2026. Catherine leads the Croatian work package with a €185,000 budget, collaborating with Elena Ferretti and teams in France and Greece. Year 1 of 4 and her largest EU grant to date.
- **Adriatic Cetacean Acoustic Survey**: Acoustic monitoring of dolphin and whale populations in the central Adriatic. Tomo's PhD project under her supervision, funded by an €18,000 internal university grant.
- Professional roles: invited reviewer for Marine Ecology Progress Letters; scientific committee member for the Mediterranean Marine Symposium 2026 in Barcelona; mentor to two PhD students.
- Side income: occasional environmental impact assessment consulting for coastal development projects, typically €2,000 to €5,000 a year, including subcontract work for a US environmental consultancy.

## Interests & Hobbies

- Open-water swimming year-round, valued for the sensory immersion more than the exercise: the cold shock in winter, the Mediterranean warmth in late summer, the way sound changes underwater.
- The gajeta restoration with Marko: grief made productive and love made tangible. Hull work is done; paint and rigging remain. Some weekends the boat gets an hour, some nothing; the process is the point, not the timeline.
- Hiking the coastal mountains, usually alone and restoratively so. The occasional hike with Marko or Isabella trades meditative silence for conversation, and she values both modes.
- Reading split between registers: Rachel Carson's "The Sea Around Us" annually as both science and literature, marine science journals, Mediterranean history, and Isabel Allende for the permission to feel more than the evidence supports.
- Cooking, especially Spanish-influenced dishes: seafood paella with the socarrat crunched just right, gazpacho, and cochinillo asado for special occasions.
- Solo sailing on Marko's spare boat, reading the Dalmatian coastline the way other people read cityscapes.

## Preferences

- Food: Mediterranean diet by default and preference. Fresh fish bought at the Riva market by what is fresh rather than what is cheap, olive oil from one Dalmatian producer she has tested over years, seasonal vegetables and legumes. Avoids processed food and packs her own field meals: sardines, bread, cheese, fruit.
- Drink: Spanish red wine, especially Rioja and Ribera del Duero Tempranillo; occasional mezcal in social settings. Café con leche on the balcony is the morning ritual, no exceptions unless at sea.
- Music: flamenco, Portuguese fado, classical (Chopin, Debussy), and ambient oceanic soundscapes for focus. She plays music softly and finds loud environments actively unpleasant; Spanish guitar played live in a small room is a particular joy.
- News and reading: El País and RTVE in Spanish, Nature and the Marine Biology Updates newsletter for science, Research Europe for EU policy. Podcasts: "The Dive Podcast", "Marine Conservation Chat", and the science episodes of "In Our Time".
- Gifts: practical and meaningful only: quality dive gear, nautical books, good Dalmatian olive oil, handmade ceramics. No perfume, no jewelry, nothing mass-produced. Her own gift-giving is precise down to the specific olive oil producer.
- Style and aesthetics: natural materials that survive salt, wind, and heat. Linen, cotton, muted earth tones, minimal jewelry beyond a watch bought in Barcelona and the small coral pendant from her father. Drawn to handmade objects that carry evidence of their origin.
- Spending: careful and guilt-free on essentials (quality food, dive equipment, research-adjacent travel) and close to nothing elsewhere. She dislikes browsing but enjoys the precision of selecting exactly the right thing.
- Travel: almost entirely work-driven; she extracts personal pleasure from professional trips with surgical efficiency and packs light. Within Croatia she prefers the car to the bus and the boat to the car.
- Decompression: the balcony bookends most days. Friday evening cooking alone, wine open and flamenco on the speaker, closes the working week, and a swim after a hard day resets her nervous system like nothing on land.
- Sensory world: she reads water conditions by feel before instruments confirm them, evaluates ceramics and fabrics by touch, and treats the Mediterranean light as data.
- Pet peeves: premature publication, prestige-driven funding talk, boats anchoring on Posidonia meadows, processed vessel food, being asked why she lives in Croatia rather than Spain, loud networking events, people who treat introversion as a problem to fix, and unreliable field equipment.
